About Muckraking

The term 'muckraker' was first coined during the Progressive Era of the early 20th century. Muckrakers were investigative journalists who targeted government officials and corporations for their misdeeds and corruption. These journalists often called for sweeping reforms in the interest of protecting the public and workers.

The Progressive Era and muckrakers rose in response to the excess and corruption of the Gilded Age during the late 19th century. Many people became concerned at how corporate monopolies were abusing workers, consumers, and the environment all while the government did nothing to stop these power grabs. How could a government allow its people to be at the mercy of for-profit institutions? Simple, the government itself had been bought by the very corporations they were charged with regulating. This should all start to sound familiar.


Notable Muckrakers

Upton Sinclair
Upton Sinclair wrote many books criticizing the abuse and exploitation of people by corporations. His most famous work is The Jungle which is about the horrible working conditions in the Chicago meatpacking industry.

Nellie Bly
Early in her career, Nellie Bly went undercover as a psychiatric patient at a notoriously harsh pychiatric hospital. She remained undercover for ten days in order to gather as much information and evidence of the hospital staff's abusive behavior.

Louis Brandeis
Louis Brandeis was an outspoken critic of monopolies, big banks, and big business. His critiques of corporations are collected in his book Other People's Money and How the Bankers Use It. He was a lifelong public advocate, eventually bringing that advocacy to the Supreme Court as an Associate Justice.

Ida M. Tarbell
Ida Tarbell was an early investigative journalist and wrote extensively about the 'trusts' that controlled much of the American economy during the late 19th and early 20th centuries. She was a prolific writer and was an expert at breaking down complex subjects into easily understable articles. Her work helped influence the creation the Clayton Anti-trust Act.
